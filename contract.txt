2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 “Activation” means a customer order that is submitted through an approved process, installed or activated if required by the applicable program, validated by the applicable Carrier, and credited by that Carrier to Company.

2.2 “Authorized Channel” means the sales or marketing method expressly approved for Contractor in the applicable Compensation Exhibit, including, where applicable, door-to-door, events, retail, call center, referral, online, or another channel specifically listed there.

2.3 “Carrier” means any telecommunications or related service provider whose products or services Contractor is authorized to market under an applicable Compensation Exhibit.

2.4 “Chargeback” means any reversal, clawback, debit, adjustment, denial, cancellation-related reversal, fraud-related reversal, short-pay, non-pay, or recoupment imposed by a Carrier or otherwise applied to an Activation or commission.

2.5 “Compensation Exhibit” means a program-specific exhibit, schedule, or addendum setting out the applicable Carrier, Authorized Channel, Territory, compensation method, chargeback rules, and related program terms.

2.6 “Confidential Information” means all non-public information relating to Company, any Carrier, compensation structures, pricing, customer information, systems, scripts, training, recruiting, operations, and business methods.

2.7 “Customer Data” means any information relating to an actual or prospective customer, including names, contact information, addresses, order details, service preferences, and any other personal or proprietary information.

2.8 “Net Commission” means the amount, if any, actually received and retained by Company for an Activation after deductions, reversals, Chargebacks, holdbacks, reserves, offsets, processing fees, and Carrier-imposed adjustments.

2.9 “Program Documents” means the Compensation Exhibits, carrier rules, approved scripts, approved marketing materials, compliance notices, acknowledgments, and other written program-specific requirements communicated by Company.

2.10 “Territory” means the geography, market, zip code area, account type, customer class, or other scope restriction stated in the applicable Compensation Exhibit or Program Documents.

2.11 “Work Product” means any materials, scripts, content, lead notes, reports, training items, deliverables, or other materials created by Contractor specifically for Company or for a Company-authorized program.

3.6 Contractor is solely responsible for all business expenses, including transportation, devices, internet, permits, insurance, training time, marketing costs, and supplies, unless Company expressly agrees otherwise in writing.

3.7 Unless a specific Compensation Exhibit expressly says otherwise, Contractor may perform services for others, provided Contractor does not violate this Agreement, misuse Confidential Information, create a direct conflict with active Company programs, or market through unauthorized channels.

3.8 Contractor may not appoint sub-contractors, sub-agents, team members, or other representatives to perform under this Agreement unless Company first approves them in writing. Contractor remains fully responsible for all acts and omissions of any approved personnel.

6. COMPENSATION

6.1 Contractor will be compensated only as described in the applicable Compensation Exhibit. There is no salary, hourly pay, draw, minimum guarantee, or expense reimbursement unless expressly stated in writing by Company.

6.2 Company has no obligation to pay Contractor for an Activation unless and until Company actually receives and retains the corresponding compensation from the applicable Carrier.

6.3 Unless a Compensation Exhibit states otherwise, Company will pay approved amounts due to Contractor within five (5) business days after Company’s receipt of the applicable Carrier payment and after Company has completed any ordinary reconciliation, reserve, offset, fraud, quality-control, or compliance review.

6.4 Contractor is responsible for one hundred percent (100%) of all Chargebacks attributable to Activations, conduct, or submissions generated by Contractor or Contractor’s approved personnel.

6.5 Company may offset Chargebacks, reserves, losses, damages, fees, refunds, penalties, returns, and any other amounts owed by Contractor against any sums otherwise payable to Contractor under any program or Compensation Exhibit. If amounts owed by Contractor exceed amounts otherwise payable, Contractor must pay the balance to Company within fifteen (15) days after written demand.

6.6 Company may hold back up to fifteen percent (15%) of gross commissions as a reserve against Chargebacks, fraud, cancellations, quality issues, or reconciliation. Company may increase, decrease, release, or continue reserves in light of Contractor’s history, complaint volume, chargeback profile, audit findings, or Carrier requirements.

6.7 Company may withhold, suspend, reduce, deny, offset, or permanently forfeit unpaid compensation, in whole or in part, upon a good-faith determination that any of the following has occurred or is reasonably suspected:
(a) fraudulent, fabricated, duplicate, unauthorized, or non-consensual orders;
(b) false, altered, incomplete, or misleading application, onboarding, order, tax, or customer information;
(c) sales made outside an Authorized Channel or outside an authorized Territory;
(d) use of unauthorized pricing, promotions, scripts, claims, or marketing materials;
(e) customer cancellation, non-pay, return, rescission, or Carrier non-credit;
(f) Chargeback risk or actual Chargeback;
(g) violation of law, Program Documents, Carrier rules, data-protection obligations, or compliance requirements;
(h) customer complaint, regulatory inquiry, Carrier complaint, internal investigation, or audit relating to Contractor’s conduct or submissions;
(i) misuse of Customer Data, Confidential Information, or Company/Carrier brand assets;
(j) failure to maintain required permits, licenses, registrations, or eligibility; or
(k) any act or omission by Contractor that causes or is likely to cause financial loss, reputational harm, legal exposure, or program disruption to Company or a Carrier.

6.8 Company may continue any withholding or suspension of unpaid compensation while it investigates the matter. If the issue is not substantiated, Company will release any undisputed amount otherwise properly payable. If the issue is substantiated, Company may permanently deny or forfeit the affected compensation and may recover any related losses through offset, invoice, or other lawful means.

6.9 Contractor must submit any commission dispute in writing within thirty (30) days after the applicable payment date. Any dispute not raised within that period is waived.

6.10 Company owes no commission for orders or activity that are fraudulent, duplicated, cancelled, non-pay, out-of-territory, out-of-channel, non-compliant, not credited to Company by the applicable Carrier, or otherwise disqualified under this Agreement or the applicable Program Documents.

6.11 After termination, Contractor remains eligible only for commissions on Activations that were fully submitted before termination, later credited and paid to Company, and not subject to Chargebacks, reserves, offsets, withholding, denial, forfeiture, or other deductions. Company may continue post-termination reconciliation and may offset post-termination amounts against any outstanding balance owed by Contractor.

6.12 The compensation stated in the applicable Compensation Exhibit(s) is the sole compensation owed to Contractor.

7. CONDUCT, LEGAL COMPLIANCE, AND CUSTOMER PROTECTION

7.1 Contractor shall act lawfully, honestly, professionally, and in a manner that protects customers, Company, and Carriers.

7.2 The following are material breaches of this Agreement:
(a) submitting or assisting with fraudulent, fabricated, duplicate, or unauthorized orders;
(b) using false, altered, or misleading customer information;
(c) selling outside an Authorized Channel or Territory;
(d) quoting unauthorized pricing, offers, or terms;
(e) misrepresenting Company, a Carrier, a program, or Contractor’s authority;
(f) contacting a Carrier without authorization;
(g) using unapproved marketing materials, websites, domains, social accounts, scripts, or brand assets;
(h) retaining, sharing, exporting, or misusing Customer Data;
(i) violating do-not-call, door-to-door, telemarketing, privacy, recording, or consumer-protection laws;
(j) impersonation, forgery, deceptive inducement, or falsified consent;
(k) harassment, threats, discrimination, or abusive conduct toward customers or other personnel; or
(l) any conduct that jeopardizes a Carrier relationship, Company program, customer trust, or Company’s reputation.

7.3 Contractor is solely responsible for complying with all federal, state, and local laws applicable to Contractor’s activities, including laws relating to telemarketing, consumer solicitation, door-to-door sales, advertising, privacy, recording, identification badges, and permits.

7.4 Contractor must promptly notify Company of any customer complaint, regulator inquiry, Carrier inquiry, cease-and-desist demand, permit issue, or investigation relating to services performed under this Agreement.

7.5 Company may suspend Contractor’s program access, onboarding status, commissions, or payments while investigating suspected fraud, legal violations, policy violations, customer harm, or data-security incidents.

8. CUSTOMER DATA, CONFIDENTIALITY, AND INFORMATION SECURITY

8.1 Contractor may use Customer Data only to perform authorized services under this Agreement and only through approved systems and workflows.

8.2 Unless Company expressly authorizes otherwise in writing, Contractor may not store Customer Data in personal notebooks, personal cloud drives, screenshots, unapproved CRMs, personal spreadsheets, or personal messaging apps.

8.3 Contractor may not sell, share, transfer, remarket, or use Customer Data for any purpose other than approved performance under this Agreement.

8.4 Contractor must notify Company immediately, and in no event later than twenty-four (24) hours after discovery, of any suspected or actual unauthorized access, disclosure, loss, compromise, or misuse of Customer Data, Confidential Information, or Company systems.

8.5 Contractor shall keep Confidential Information strictly confidential during the term of this Agreement and for three (3) years after termination.

8.6 Contractor may use Confidential Information solely to perform authorized services under this Agreement.

8.7 Confidential Information includes compensation plans, pricing, Carrier terms, customer information, training, recruiting methods, scripts, workflows, operations, systems, vendor relationships, business strategy, and non-public program rules.

8.8 Confidential Information does not include information that Contractor can prove:
(a) became public through no fault of Contractor;
(b) was lawfully known to Contractor before disclosure by Company; or
(c) was lawfully obtained from a third party without breach of duty.

8.9 If Contractor is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, Contractor shall, to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify Company and reasonably cooperate with Company’s efforts to limit disclosure.

8.10 Upon Company’s request or termination of this Agreement, Contractor must immediately stop using Customer Data and Confidential Information and return or securely destroy them, except to the extent retention is required by law.

11. NON-SOLICITATION AND NON-CIRCUMVENTION

11.1 During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months after termination, Contractor shall not knowingly solicit a customer obtained through Contractor’s work under this Agreement for the purpose of cancelling, transferring, replacing, or competing against the applicable Company-authorized service, except with Company’s written consent or as part of authorized Company activity.

11.2 During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months after termination, Contractor shall not directly solicit for competing telecom or related programs any employee or contractor of Company with whom Contractor had material business contact through Company, except through general public recruiting not directed at such person.

11.3 During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months after termination, Contractor shall not knowingly bypass Company in order to obtain direct access to, or direct economic benefit from, a Carrier program first introduced to Contractor through Company, where such conduct would use Company Confidential Information or interfere with Company’s existing business relationships.

11.4 Contractor agrees that a breach of this Section, or of Sections 8, 9, or 10, may cause harm that is difficult to measure and that Company may seek temporary, preliminary, and permanent injunctive relief in addition to any other remedies.

12. TERM, SUSPENSION, AND TERMINATION

12.1 This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated as provided herein.

12.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time upon thirty (30) days’ written notice.

12.3 Company may suspend Contractor immediately, or terminate this Agreement immediately, upon any of the following:
(a) fraud or suspected fraud;
(b) material breach of this Agreement or Program Documents;
(c) violation of law or Carrier rules;
(d) customer harm, deception, or data misuse;
(e) false or misleading application, tax, onboarding, or order information;
(f) failure to maintain required permits, licenses, registrations, or eligibility;
(g) loss, suspension, or change of a Carrier program affecting Contractor; or
(h) conduct reasonably likely to damage Company, a Carrier, or customers.

12.4 Upon termination:
(a) Contractor shall immediately stop representing Company and stop using Company or Carrier materials;
(b) all licenses granted to Contractor end immediately;
(c) Contractor shall cease access to Company systems and return or destroy Company property and Confidential Information;
(d) Company may continue reconciliation, holdbacks, reserves, offsets, withholding, denial, forfeiture, and Chargeback recovery; and
(e) provisions that by their nature should survive shall survive.

12.5 A Carrier program or Compensation Exhibit may be ended or changed independently of this Agreement. If that happens, Contractor’s rights under that specific program end or change accordingly, without affecting the rest of this Agreement unless Company states otherwise.

14.4 The arbitration shall take place in the county in Ohio where Company maintains its principal office on the Effective Date, unless the parties agree otherwise in writing.

14.5 The arbitrator may award any remedy available under applicable law and this Agreement, except that the arbitrator may not consolidate claims of different parties without the written consent of all affected parties.

14.6 The arbitrator may award costs and reasonable attorneys’ fees to the prevailing party to the extent permitted by law and the applicable arbitration rules.

14.7 Either party may seek temporary, preliminary, or permanent injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction in Ohio to protect Confidential Information, Customer Data, intellectual property, or restrictive covenants pending or in aid of arbitration.
